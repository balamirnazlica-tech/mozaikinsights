A tab-by-tab guide to reading this dashboard, designed for department heads and VPs. Use it to run weekly pipeline reviews, challenge stage accuracy, and spot risks before they cost you the quarter.

Tab Overview

The four KPI cards show Open Opportunities (active deals across all stages), Gross Pipeline (sum of deal values where a number exists), Weighted Pipeline (each value x probability — your best forecast), and Data Completeness (avg % of key fields filled).

The funnel visualization shows count, gross, and weighted value per stage. A healthy funnel is wider at the top. If BOFU dominates, short-term revenue looks strong but Q3+ pipeline is at risk.

Action: If weighted BOFU > 3x weighted TOFU, escalate prospecting immediately.

Tab Funnel Definitions

TOFU = discovery/interest (5–25% probability). MOFU = qualified with proposal (30–70%). BOFU = closing, terms agreed (75–99%). Use these criteria to challenge stage placement during reviews.

A deal without confirmed budget should not be in MOFU. A deal at 100% probability should be moved to “Won” immediately — leaving it in BOFU inflates the pipeline.

Action: In each review, pick 2–3 deals and verify stage vs. criteria.

Tab Rep Performance

Scoreboard with each rep’s deal count, stage split, gross and weighted pipeline, average deal size, and data fill %. Use it to spot uneven workload, low data fill (coaching signal), and concentration risk (one rep holding most value).

Action: Flag any rep with data fill < 70% for a 1:1 data-quality review.

Tab BOFU Close Watch

Deals expected to close this quarter, sorted by weighted value. Highlighted rows = ≥85% probability. Start every weekly review here: which deals moved? Which slipped? Any stuck ≥14 days?

Action: Any BOFU deal without movement for 2 weeks → require a concrete next-step with a date, or downgrade to MOFU.

Tab At-Risk / Stuck

Deals with probability <20%, missing next steps, or flagged blockers (Ghosting, Budget, Operation, Time). These are pipeline “dead weight” that distort your forecast.

Action: Every deal here for 2+ weeks without a next step → re-stage to TOFU or mark Lost. No exceptions.

Tab Data Quality

Field fill rates across all 108 deals, plus rows with free-text values that can’t be forecasted. Your forecast is only as accurate as the data. Key gaps: 33% of deals have no numeric value, 55% missing close dates.

Action: Set a team target of ≥85% data fill. Tie it to the weekly cadence.

Funnel stage definitions — tailored to Mozaik Design

Mozaik's enterprise sales cycle typically involves an architect/specifier, a client/owner, and a product package (furniture, lighting, fabrics). Here's a proposed definition of each stage that maps cleanly to what the team is already tracking.

TOFU Top of Funnel — Discovery / interest

A qualified target where we've made contact, but there is no confirmed brief, budget, or timeline yet. We are building relationships with the architect or owner.

Entry criteria:
  • Target company + contact identified
  • We've had at least one meaningful touchpoint (meeting, site visit, showroom, intro mail)
  • Broad product fit is plausible (hospitality, office, yacht, retail)
Exit to MOFU when:
  • Specific project brief exists (name, location, scope)
  • Architect engaged or RFP received
  • Indicative budget range known
Typical probability: 5–25%

MOFU Middle of Funnel — Qualification & proposal

Active project with a defined scope. We are selecting products, pricing, and competing with alternatives. A formal proposal has been sent or is being prepared.

Entry criteria:
  • Project brief confirmed and quantified (€ value)
  • Architect actively specifying our products
  • Decision-maker known and reachable
  • Proposal sent or scheduled
Exit to BOFU when:
  • Proposal accepted verbally or budget confirmed
  • Competitive set narrowed to us + 1, or we're sole source
  • Order / contract being drafted; only sign-off & terms remain
Typical probability: 30–70%

BOFU Bottom of Funnel — Closing

Commercial terms are agreed in principle. We're waiting on signature, purchase order, or down-payment. Slippage here is usually operational, not competitive.

Entry criteria (all must be true):
  • Proposal sent & approved
  • Budget explicitly confirmed
  • Decision-maker committed
  • A compelling event exists (opening date, season, move-in)
Exit to Won when:
  • Signed order / contract received
  • Down-payment received (or PO issued for corporate)
Typical probability: 75–99%
Recommended probability bands. Right now probabilities are free-form (0.01 → 1.0) which makes forecasting noisy. Standardize to: TOFU 10 / 20 · MOFU 30 / 50 / 70 · BOFU 80 / 90 / 100. Anything at 100% should be moved to a "Won" stage, not left in BOFU.

    What's missing from the funnel

    • No "Won" or "Lost" stages. The file ends at BOFU, so closed deals, win rate and cycle time cannot be calculated. The weekly notes mention wins (LoomGames, Pekin, Hakkı Yıldız) but they're not in the staged sheets.
    • No lead source / industry dimension. We can't tell which channel (architect referral, existing client, outbound, showroom) or vertical (hospitality / office / yacht / retail / bank) is converting best.
    • No stage-entry dates. Without "entered TOFU on", "entered MOFU on", we cannot measure velocity or time-in-stage — the "Sales Velocity" column exists but is empty in every BOFU row.
    • Close dates are ~55% missing and many that exist are free text ("end of april", "soon", "belirsiz", "2027"). This blocks any time-phased forecast.
    • Value is missing or text on 33% of deals (36/108). TOFU in particular is unusable for pipeline value — only 1 of 44 TOFU deals has a €number.
    • No "compelling event" captured below BOFU. The compelling-event column is BOFU-only, but MOFU/TOFU also need it (season, opening, move-in) to judge urgency.
    • No owner of the next step. Next step text is there, but there's no "who" and "by when" — so it can't drive a weekly cadence.
    • No products / quantities / margin. We know €value but not what's being sold — impossible to forecast stock, GM%, or category mix.
    • No lost reasons. The notes hint at Ghosting, Operation, Time — these should be structured drop-down reasons on every lost deal.
    • OUTBOUND sheet has no date / status. We can't tell whether a target architect was contacted last week or last year.

    Top insights from what is there

    • The pipeline is top-heavy on Zeynep and İrem. Together they carry ~60% of opportunity count. Burçin holds the biggest hospitality whales (Hillside Bodrum €1.2M, Divan Göcek €341K). Elif's book is the thinnest and 3 of her deals are flagged "ghosting".
    • €7.1M weighted in BOFU vs. €1.8M in MOFU vs. €0.2M in TOFU — this funnel is severely inverted. That's fine short-term (Q2 will be strong) but means April/May wins won't be replaced. Pipeline risk Q3+
    • A handful of deals drive most of the value. The top 5 BOFU deals = ~€4.1M weighted, i.e. ~58% of BOFU. Concentration risk is real: any one slipping moves the quarter.
    • Proposal Sent is filled 82% of the time but Budget Confirmed only 66% — the team is quoting before confirming budget, which explains some of the <20% probability deals (e.g. Aydın Kuruyemiş €430K p=5%, Medipol €720K p=5%).
    • Architect Engaged = Yes on 70/83 filled deals (84%). Architects are the strongest signal — which argues the OUTBOUND sheet (a list of architect firms) is the right prospecting engine, but it needs statuses to be actionable.
    • 4 deals are already at 100% probability (Divan Talya Upsell, Barut Anda, Kundu Voyage, Numarine VIP). They should move to a "Won — awaiting PO/payment" column this week, not sit in BOFU.
    • Closing risk cluster: 10 deals at ≤20% probability are still labelled BOFU/MOFU. Either re-stage to TOFU or mark Lost — leaving them inflates pipeline optics.
    • Outbound coverage is uneven. Named architect targets: Burçin 14, Elif 11, İrem 9, Zeynep 0 (she has 4 placeholder rows with no company; Elif has 2 extra empty rows too). Outbound to architects is the cheapest path to TOFU — close that gap.

    Recommendations before CRM adoption

    1. Fix the schema. Every row must have: Rep · Account · Project · Stage · Value (€, numeric only) · Close Date (date only) · Probability (fixed bands) · Stage-entered date · Next step owner · Next step due date · Lost reason (if lost).
    2. Add Won and Lost stages. A funnel without them cannot calculate win rate, avg cycle time, or average deal size — the three numbers any sales leader needs.
    3. Standardize probabilities to the bands in the Funnel-definitions tab. Remove 0.01 / 0.51 / 0.97 style noise.
    4. One language for boolean fields. Pick Yes / No / Unknown (or evet / hayır / bilinmiyor). Right now there's a mix of evet, yes, hayır, no, "belirsiz", "not yet", "bütçe üzerindeyiz" — none of which can be filtered or reported on.
    5. Upgrade OUTBOUND to a true prospecting list. Add Status, Last-touch date, Next-touch date, and convert to TOFU when a project is known.
    6. Track lost reason as a drop-down: Ghosting, Budget, Competitor, Timing, Operation, Price, Product mismatch. Gives you coaching loops instead of anecdotes.
    7. Add industry and source fields. Hospitality / Office / Yacht / Retail / Bank · Architect referral / Existing client / Outbound / Showroom / Inbound. These two alone unlock 80% of useful pipeline analytics.
    8. Weekly cadence: every Monday, every rep must (a) advance or disqualify any deal ≥14 days without movement, (b) refresh close dates in BOFU, (c) add 2 new TOFU opportunities. This single rule keeps the funnel healthy.
