A tab-by-tab guide to reading this dashboard, designed for department heads. Use it to run weekly sales reviews and spot performance gaps quickly.

Tab Overview

KPI Cards

The five KPI cards show Total Revenue (€49.95M), Units Sold (2,554), Total Customers (1,733), Net New Customers (867), and Avg Order Value (€20k). Toggle between Revenue and Units Sold using the dropdown. Filter by time period: All Time, FY 2025, FY 2026 YTD, Q1 2026, or Last 6 months.

Action: If Net New Customers drops below 50/month, escalate lead generation immediately.

Tab Departments

Department-level revenue breakdown. Retail leads at 54% (€26.98M), followed by Enterprise at 31% (€15.42M), Lighting at 15% (€7.29M), and Marketing at <1%.

Action: Retail carries the business. If any single department drops below 20% of its trailing 3-month average, flag for review.

Tab Sales Reps

Individual rep performance by revenue and order volume. Zeynep (Enterprise) leads revenue at €6.97M — 40% ahead of #2. Ceren M. (Marketing) leads order volume with 496 orders but ranks lower in revenue, signalling high-volume, lower-ticket deals.

Action: Any rep with revenue below €2M over the period needs a pipeline review. Compare volume vs. value to identify reps who may need upsell coaching.

Tab Customers

Client-level data including order history and spend. Use this to identify top accounts and at-risk relationships where spend is declining.

Action: Flag any top-20 customer with a quarter-over-quarter spend decline >15%.

Tab Deep Analysis

Cross-cutting insights combining department, rep, and customer data. Use for strategic planning and board-level reporting.

Action: Review monthly for trends. Bring the top 3 insights to the leadership meeting.

Weekly Review Checklist for Heads

  • Department trends — compare each department to its trailing 3-month average; flag any >10% drop
  • Rep performance — identify bottom 2 reps by revenue; schedule coaching or pipeline reviews
  • Top customers — verify top-10 accounts are on track; flag any with declining orders
  • Volume vs. value — check if high-volume reps are leaving money on the table (low AOV)
  • Net new customers — are we adding at least 50 new customers per month?
  • Filters — toggle to Units Sold view to catch volume trends that revenue might mask
